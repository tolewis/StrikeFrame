## Image 1: AdobeStock_372953083.jpeg
**Name:** sportfisher-running

**Scene:** Blue sportfisher at speed, cutting through open ocean. White spray at the bow. Tower and outriggers visible. Clear blue sky with light clouds.

**Composition analysis:**
- Subject position: CENTER-RIGHT (boat fills right 60% of frame)
- Horizon line: MIDDLE (~50% height). Clean horizon separating sky and water.
- Bright regions: Sky (top half), spray (center-right)
- Dark regions: Water (bottom half, especially bottom-left)
- Negative space: LEFT side — open sky above, open water below. Clean and uncluttered.

**Layout decision:** `editorial-left`
- Text goes on the left where the open sky and water create natural negative space.
- Headline above the horizon in the sky zone (y ~300 on 1080px canvas).
- Subhead below the horizon in the darker water zone.
- The boat acts as visual counterweight on the right — classic balance.

**Overlay strategy:** Heavier on the left (0.55 opacity) to darken the sky for white text legibility, fading to light on the right (0.15) to keep the boat vibrant.

**Mood:** Aspirational, power, purpose. "Going somewhere." This is the money shot for TOF.

**Best copy angles:** Aspiration, identity, confidence. This image says "we belong out here."

**Square crop note:** In a 1080x1080 crop, the boat stays right-of-center. Sky above, water below. Good balance. The horizon may shift slightly depending on crop position — keep headline at least 60px above the horizon band.

## Image 2: AdobeStock_133588506.jpeg
**Name:** underwater-mahi-sunlight

**Scene:** Underwater shot of a mahi-mahi (dorado) swimming through deep blue water. Sunlight piercing through the surface from above-right. Bubbles scattered in the bright zone. The fish is neon green/yellow against the deep blue.

**Composition analysis:**
- Subject position: BOTTOM-CENTER (fish at ~60-70% height)
- Horizon/surface line: TOP-RIGHT corner — turbulent white water and sun
- Bright regions: Top-right quadrant (sunlight through surface)
- Dark regions: Bottom-left quadrant (deep blue water)
- Negative space: Upper-left and lower portions of the frame. The deep blue is remarkably clean.

**Layout decision:** `centered-hero`
- Headline in the bright upper zone (y ~160) where sunlight creates a natural spotlight.
- Subhead in the dark water below the fish (y ~750).
- Fish sits between the two text zones — natural visual break.

**Overlay strategy:** Light overall (0.2-0.25 opacity). The water is already dark enough for white text in the lower zone. The upper zone needs slight darkening to maintain contrast against the bright sunlight.

**Mood:** Beauty, wonder, the underwater world. Stops the scroll because it's striking and unexpected in an ad context.

**Best copy angles:** Lifestyle hooks, "this is why we fish" energy. Not product-focused. Pure emotional pull.

**Square crop note:** In square crop, the fish may get pushed down. Adjust subheadY accordingly. The key is keeping the bright sunlight zone available for headline text.

**Name:** fisherman-rough-seas

**Scene:** Commercial fisherman in yellow rain gear and overalls, hauling a net full of fish from rough, dark-teal seas. The ocean is choppy and moody. Another vessel visible in the misty background. Overcast sky.

**Composition analysis:**
- Subject position: LEFT (fisherman fills left 50%)
- Horizon line: UPPER THIRD (~30% height). Overcast sky blending into misty ocean.
- Bright regions: The yellow rain gear (eye magnet). Overcast sky is flat gray.
- Dark regions: Ocean (bottom right), nets and boat structure
- Negative space: UPPER-RIGHT — misty sky/ocean. Some space but moody/textured.

**Layout decision:** `centered-hero`
- With the subject on the left, an `editorial-left` layout would stack text on top of the fisherman. Bad.
- Centered-hero puts headline in the upper sky (y ~160) and subhead below (y ~780) in the dark water.
- Alternatively, could explore right-aligned text, but StrikeFrame defaults handle centered better.

**Mood:** Grit, toughness, the working ocean. This is the furthest image from "aspirational leisure" — it's blue-collar fishing energy. Connects with the audience segment that fishes for a living or respects those who do.

**Best copy angles:** Resilience, reliability. "The ocean doesn't negotiate." This image demands copy that matches its weight. No cute taglines. No emojis. Straight talk.

**Square crop note:** In square crop, the fisherman stays left. The moody ocean fills the right. Top and bottom of the frame lose some sky/water — that's fine. The drama concentrates.
